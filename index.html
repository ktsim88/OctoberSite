Early Life

Agatha Christie was born on Septermber 15, 1890 in Torquay, Devon England. When she was younger, she loved to make stories as a child. She taught herself how to read and write. She married Archie Christie but they got into a fight. In 1928, they divorced and she married Max Mallowman in 1930. She always traveled with him to the Middle East which is why many of her stories are in that area.

Challenges

On December 3, 1926, her mom died which took a toll on her. She disappeared on December 3, 1926 for 11 days. It was fully public as there were many news about her. Many policement were involved. It led to Arthur Conan Doyla and Dorothy L Sayers, who were authors, to get involved to find more information about Christie. She was found at a health spa and there was no confirmed reason why she dissappeared and she never spoke about it.

Major Accomplishments

Agatha Christie accomplished many things in her lifetime which include her creation of Poirot, her expertise with poisons, and becoming the bestselling murder mystery author of all time.

Best Selling Author

Her novel that defined her career was "The Murder of Roger Ackroyd". She had a lot of sales for that book.

Her name is in the Guinness Book of Records for bestselling author of all time. There were over a hundred million books sold.

Hercule Poirot

Poirot was one of Agatha Christie's iconic characters in her books. He first appeared in "The Mystery Affair at Styles".

Poison Master

Agatha Christie passed a test in order for her to become an assistant at a pharmacy. She used her knowledge to make 85 novels about poison.
One of her novels that included poison was "Pale Horse" which had a patient that had thallium poisoning.

Major Contributions

Agatha Christie accomplished a lot and contributed to society from helping the genre to having her books into film.

Popularized Mystery

Agatha Christie’s books were very popular which changed and helped this genre. She made a lot of books that had a whodunit situation. Someone was murdered and Poirot or a different detective is called to solve a mystery. She adds suspense and builds up to show motivations of different characters.

Books to Film

Many of her books were adapted to film. One of them included "Murder on the Orient Express". Her legacy still continues to this day because of the popularity of her books.

Longest Running Play

Agatha Christie wrote a play called "The Mousetrap". It lasted for over 70 years. It ran from October 1952 until March 2020 due to Covid. It was first in Hottingham, transfered to Ambassadors Theatre, and stayed at St Martins Theatre. It's still runnning to this day.
